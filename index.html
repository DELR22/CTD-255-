This elective course guides students through the integrated digital workflow of contemporary interior design practice, using a design-process framework. Students follow instructor-produced video tutorials to complete an interior design case study, tracing the design process from AI-assisted concept generation through spatial planning, technical documentation, and final presentation.

AutoCAD is introduced as a tool for schematic block planning and area calculation, while Autodesk Revit serves as the primary environment for all interior modeling and technical documentation. Twinmotion is used to develop real-time 3D visualizations directly linked to the Revit model, and Adobe Photoshop, combined with AI image-refining tools, is used for post-production rendering and designing final presentation graphics and layouts.

The standard that applies is: every 1 credit hour, students should spend roughly 2 hours on outside coursework. This translates to 6 hours of independent work per week, which totals 96 hours of work in four months.
In a Month (4-Week) Format

Academic Policies
Course Policies
Missed Coursework & Resubmissions
1
All assignments have published deadlines that align with the four design process stages. Deadlines exist because each stage builds on the previous one — missing them affects your ability to complete subsequent work.
2
Late work is accepted up to seven days past the deadline with a deduction of ten points per day. Work submitted more than seven days late receives a zero and cannot be resubmitted.
3
If you need an extension, contact the instructor in writing before the deadline — or within 48 hours if an emergency prevents this. Extensions are not guaranteed but will be considered for documented illness, family emergencies, or serious extenuating circumstances.
4
Incomplete submissions receive partial credit only for the components that are present. They are not eligible for resubmission. Submitting incomplete work to hold a deadline slot does not protect your resubmission rights — only complete submissions do.
5
Complete, on-time submissions are eligible for resubmission. This course treats revision as a normal and expected part of the design process, not as a second chance reserved for special cases.
6
You have ten days from the date graded feedback is returned to submit a revised version. Resubmissions after this window will not be reviewed.
7
Every resubmission requires a Revision Statement — a short written explanation identifying the feedback you received, the changes you made, and why you made them. Resubmissions without a Revision Statement will not be reviewed. Revisions that only make superficial corrections without engaging the feedback will not improve your grade.
8
Resubmissions can improve your grade by a maximum of 15% of the assignment's total point value. For example, a 150/200 on the Revit Model can improve to a maximum of 180/200 upon resubmission. Each assignment may only be resubmitted once.
9
The Collaborative Process Board is not subject to standard resubmission rules. Low scores on an early check-in can be offset by demonstrated improvement in later entries — the final assessment considers your growth across all four stages.
10
If you fall behind, communicate early. Students who disengage without contact and seek resolution at the end of the term will have limited options.
Attendance
1
This is a virtual, primarily asynchronous course. Most of your work happens independently through instructor-produced video tutorials that you complete at your own pace. Tutorial content is available starting the first day of class — students are expected to begin the tutorials immediately and progress steadily through each stage. You are expected to manage your time responsibly and advance through the material without waiting to be prompted.
2
The course meets twice a week via video call at times agreed upon with the group at the start of the term. These sessions are dedicated to reviewing project development, providing feedback, and offering design guidance — they are not lectures.
3
Attendance at synchronous sessions is not mandatory. This course is designed for autonomous learners who can self-direct their process. You will not be penalized for missing a session.
4
Missing sessions means missing feedback. Synchronous meetings are the primary opportunity to receive direct, real-time input on your work before the next submission deadline.
5
To participate in a session, you must have work to show. Sessions are project reviews, not open office hours. Students who join without progress to discuss will be asked to return when they have work ready.
6
If you cannot attend a scheduled session, no notification is required. However, if you are at a critical project stage and need feedback before a deadline, contact the instructor in advance to arrange an alternative.
7
Repeated absence will not affect your grade directly, but it will likely affect the quality of your submissions. The feedback offered in these sessions informs the Conceptual Alignment and Methodological Integrity dimensions on which your work is evaluated.
8
Video tutorials must be completed in sequence. The asynchronous content follows the same four-stage design process structure as the assignments. Skipping ahead or working out of order will undermine the coherence of your project.
9
The course does not track login activity or time spent on tutorials as a measure of participation. Your engagement is assessed entirely through the quality and completeness of your submitted work.
10
Autonomous behavior is both expected and respected. You are trusted to manage your schedule, complete the tutorials, attend sessions when they serve your process, and take ownership of your learning.
