Design Challenge

The Brief

This project requires students to retrofit a six-story industrial building into a contemporary student residence. The structural system — a concrete frame with 18"×24" columns on a 22'×29' grid — was engineered for industrial load patterns, not residential living. Every design decision must negotiate between existing constraints and the demands of a livable, code-compliant multi-unit community.

The floor plate measures 49' × 132', yielding 6,468 square feet per level. Two emergency stair towers, encased in structural concrete walls and positioned at diagonally opposite corners of the building, are fixed elements that govern all circulation logic on every floor. These towers — and the elevator that must be integrated into the service core — are the three non-negotiable anchors of the plan.

Floor-to-floor heights vary by location: the 16-foot ground level creates generous volume for amenity programming, while the 11'–6" bays on floors 2 through 6 demand careful coordination of mechanical systems, ceiling depths, and minimum residential clearances. The flat concrete slab roof offers an opportunity for student outdoor programming, with occupancy limited to 50% of the total rooftop area by applicable code restriction.

Program Notes

Design Parameters & Constraints

01 All unit areas are net interior, measured to the interior face of demising walls. Wall thickness is not included within individual unit figures and will be absorbed within the gross floor area during schematic layout.
02 The 45-unit program totals 26,545 sf against 26,440 sf of available net area per floor — a 105 sf total overage resolved through precise corridor geometry and demising wall coordination at schematic design.
03 Unit distribution across Floors 2–6 is a student design variable. No single floor may assign more than 5,288 sf of unit area. Students should confirm that each floor's unit selection satisfies this constraint before proceeding to Revit modeling.
04 An accessible elevator is a required life-safety and ADA compliance element. It is accounted for separately on Floor 1 (80 sf, including pit or overhead machine space) and on each residential floor (50 sf per level).
05 The 50% rooftop occupancy limit reflects applicable code and zoning restrictions for rooftop amenity areas. Per IBC §1015 (Guards), a minimum 5'-0" setback from all parapet and roof-edge conditions to any occupiable programming is required, generating the 1,234 sf non-occupiable perimeter buffer shown in the rooftop program. A 42" (3'-6") code-compliant guard must be provided at all open-sided edges of the occupiable zone. All mechanical equipment must remain within the non-occupiable zone and may not project into the designated occupiable area.
06 Type D (526 sf, 1 BR / 2 BA) carries a master bathroom and a common full bathroom in a single-bedroom plan. This configuration is best suited to accessible or adaptable occupancy. Confirm design intent before developing unit layouts.
